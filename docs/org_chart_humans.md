A typical Agile software development company is organized into **Sprints** at the team level, while the broader organization follows a functional hierarchy. For a mid-sized company (approx. 50–100 people), the structure is typically divided into three distinct levels.

### **Level 1: Executive Leadership (The "Why")**

This level defines the vision, budget, and high-level strategy.

* **CEO (1):** Sets the overall company direction.
* **CTO (1):** Decides on technology infrastructure (e.g., local AI vs. Cloud).
* **Product Director / VP of Product (1):** Defines the long-term product roadmap.

---

### **Level 2: Management & Chapters (The "How well")**

These roles ensure quality and professional growth across different teams.

* **Engineering Managers (1 per 15–20 devs):** Responsible for career growth and people management.
* **Chapter Leads (1 per skill area):** Lead experts in specific fields (e.g., a "Python Chapter Lead" or "Frontend Chapter Lead").
* **Product Managers (1 per product line):** Align multiple squads toward a single business goal.

---

### **Level 3: The Agile Squad (The "How")**

This is the execution core. A standard Agile Squad is ideally **5–9 members** to maintain high communication speed.

| Role | Members | Key Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Product Owner** | 1 | Prioritizes the backlog; represents the customer. |
| **Scrum Master** | 0.5–1* | Facilitates the process; removes team blockers. |
| **Lead/Senior Developer** | 1 | Oversees architecture and code quality. |
| **Backend Developers** | 2–3 | Logic, APIs, and Database (Python/MongoDB). |
| **Frontend Developers** | 1–2 | User interface and experience (React). |
| **QA / Test Engineer** | 1 | Ensures every feature meets the "Definition of Done." |
| **UI/UX Designer** | 0.5–1* | Creates the visual layouts and user journeys. |

**Note: In many organizations, a Scrum Master or Designer may split their time between two closely related squads.*

---

### **Total Headcount Ratio (Rule of Thumb)**

If you are planning an organization, a common "balanced" ratio for the delivery side is:

* **1 Product Owner** : **1 Scrum Master** : **5–7 Developers** : **1 QA**
